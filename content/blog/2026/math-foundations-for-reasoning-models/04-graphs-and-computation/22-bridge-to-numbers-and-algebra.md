# Bridge To Numbers And Algebra

## What This Chapter Built

This chapter introduced graphs as structure.

Graphs gave us:

- nodes
- edges
- direction
- weights
- paths
- cycles
- trees
- dependency graphs
- computation graphs

> Graphs show how things are connected.

---

## What Graphs Explain Well

Graphs are good at representing structure.

They answer questions like:

- what is connected?
- what depends on what?
- can one node reach another?
- does the structure contain a cycle?
- where does data flow?

These are structural questions.

> Graphs make connection and dependency visible.

---

## Why Numbers Come Next

Many graph questions need measurement.

Examples:

```text
How many nodes are there?
How many edges are there?
How long is this path?
What is the weight of this edge?
Which route has lower cost?
```

To answer these, we need numbers.

> Numbers let us count, measure, and compare graph structure.

---

## Why Algebra Comes Next

Algebra lets us describe rules for transforming values.

Example:

```text
new value = old value + 1
```

or:

```text
total cost = edge1 + edge2 + edge3
```

Graph structure tells us what is connected.

Algebra lets us compute with the values attached to that structure.

> Algebra gives rules for manipulating quantities.

---

## From Structure To Calculation

Graphs and algebra work together.

Example:

```text
A --2-- B --4-- C
```

The graph shows the route:

```text
A to B to C
```

The numbers let us calculate the total:

```text
2 + 4 = 6
```

> Structure tells us what to combine; numbers and algebra tell us how to combine it.

---

## Bridge Summary

- Graphs represent connection and dependency.
- Numbers let us count and measure.
- Algebra lets us express transformation rules.
- Weighted graphs already show why numerical values matter.
- Computation graphs already show why algebraic rules matter.
- The next chapter builds the numerical and algebraic foundation needed for later topics.
