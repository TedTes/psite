# Rate Of Change

## Definition

Rate of change compares output change to input change.

Basic form:

```text
change in output / change in input
```

or:

```text
delta y / delta x
```

> Rate of change tells how strongly output responds to input movement.

---

## Average Rate Of Change

Average rate of change compares two points.

Example:

```text
x changes from 2 to 5
f(x) changes from 4 to 10
```

Then:

```text
change in output = 10 - 4 = 6
change in input = 5 - 2 = 3
rate of change = 6 / 3 = 2
```

> Average rate of change summarizes change across an interval.

---

## Constant Rate Of Change

For a linear function:

```text
f(x) = 2x + 1
```

the rate of change is always `2`.

Examples:

```text
x: 1 -> 2, output changes by 2
x: 5 -> 6, output changes by 2
x: 10 -> 11, output changes by 2
```

> Linear functions have constant rate of change.

---

## Changing Rate Of Change

For a nonlinear function:

```text
f(x) = x^2
```

the rate of change depends on where you look.

Examples:

```text
from 1 to 2: output changes from 1 to 4
from 10 to 11: output changes from 100 to 121
```

The second interval changes much more.

> Nonlinear functions can change at different rates in different regions.

---

## Rate Of Change And Slope

On a graph, rate of change appears as slope.

```text
steeper upward -> larger positive rate
steeper downward -> larger negative rate
flat -> near zero rate
```

Slope is the geometric view.

Rate of change is the input-output view.

> Slope and rate of change describe the same relationship from different angles.

---

## Why Rate Of Change Matters

Learning requires asking:

```text
If I change this value, how much does the result change?
```

Small rate:

```text
output barely changes
```

Large rate:

```text
output changes strongly
```

> Rate of change measures sensitivity.

---

## Rate Is Not The Same As Value

A function value tells where the output is.

A rate of change tells how the output is moving.

Example:

```text
value = current height
rate = how fast height is changing
```

Two points can have the same value but different rates of change.

> Value describes position; rate describes movement.

---

## Final Summary

- Rate of change compares output change to input change.
- Average rate of change compares two points.
- Linear functions have constant rate of change.
- Nonlinear functions can have changing rates.
- Rate of change is sensitivity.
- Derivatives measure local rate of change.
