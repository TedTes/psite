# Union

## Definition

The union of two sets contains every element that belongs to either set.

Example:

```text
A = {1, 2}
B = {2, 3}
```

Union:

```text
A union B = {1, 2, 3}
```

> Union combines membership from both sets.

---

## Union Keeps Distinct Elements

The element `2` appears in both sets:

```text
A = {1, 2}
B = {2, 3}
```

But the union contains it once:

```text
{1, 2, 3}
```

Sets do not count repeated membership as duplicate elements.

> Union follows set membership, not repeated listing.

---

## Union As OR

Union is similar to logical OR.

An object belongs to:

```text
A union B
```

if:

```text
it belongs to A OR it belongs to B
```

Example:

```text
2 belongs to A
2 belongs to B
```

So `2` belongs to the union.

> Union generalizes OR over set membership.

---

## Why Union Matters

Union lets us combine domains or categories.

Example:

```text
Even = {2, 4, 6}
Odd = {1, 3, 5}
```

Union:

```text
{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
```

This creates a larger collection from smaller collections.

> Union builds larger sets from alternatives.

---

## Final Summary

- Union contains elements from either set.
- Duplicate appearances do not matter.
- Union behaves like OR for membership.
- Union combines domains or categories.
- Union creates a larger set from multiple sets.
