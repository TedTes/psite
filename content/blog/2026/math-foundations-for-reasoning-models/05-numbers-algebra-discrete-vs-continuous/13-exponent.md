# Exponent

## Definition

An exponent describes repeated multiplication.

Example:

```text
2^3
```

means:

```text
2 * 2 * 2 = 8
```

> Exponent = compact notation for repeated multiplication.

---

## Base And Exponent

In:

```text
2^3
```

the base is:

```text
2
```

the exponent is:

```text
3
```

The exponent tells how many times the base is used as a factor.

> Base is what gets multiplied; exponent tells how many factors.

---

## Simple Examples

Examples:

```text
3^2 = 3 * 3 = 9
4^3 = 4 * 4 * 4 = 64
10^2 = 10 * 10 = 100
```

Exponents make repeated multiplication easier to write.

> Exponents compress repeated multiplication into a smaller expression.

---

## Exponent Of One

Any value raised to exponent `1` stays itself.

Example:

```text
5^1 = 5
x^1 = x
```

The base appears once.

> Exponent `1` means one copy of the base.

---

## Exponent Of Zero

For any nonzero base:

```text
x^0 = 1
```

Example:

```text
5^0 = 1
```

This rule keeps exponent patterns consistent.

> Any nonzero base to the power of zero equals `1`.

---

## Why Exponents Matter

Exponents describe growth patterns.

Example:

```text
2^1 = 2
2^2 = 4
2^3 = 8
2^4 = 16
```

Each step multiplies by the same base.

> Exponents represent repeated scaling.

---

## Final Summary

- An exponent represents repeated multiplication.
- The base is the value being multiplied.
- The exponent tells how many factors are used.
- `x^1 = x`.
- For nonzero `x`, `x^0 = 1`.
- Exponents describe repeated scaling and growth.
