# Closed-Form Solution

## Definition

A closed-form solution gives an answer directly using a finite expression.

Example:

```text
x + 3 = 10
```

Closed-form solution:

```text
x = 10 - 3
x = 7
```

> Closed-form solution = direct expression for the answer.

---

## Direct Computation

Closed-form solutions do not require repeated approximation.

Example:

```text
area = width * height
```

If:

```text
width = 4
height = 5
```

then:

```text
area = 4 * 5 = 20
```

The answer comes from direct substitution and calculation.

> Closed form gives a finite rule for the result.

---

## Formula As Closed Form

A formula is often a closed-form solution.

Example:

```text
distance = speed * time
```

Given speed and time, the result can be computed directly.

No search or repeated update is needed.

> A closed form packages the solution as an expression.

---

## Not Every Problem Has A Simple Closed Form

Some problems are hard to solve directly.

In those cases, we may use repeated steps to get closer to an answer.

That is an iterative solution.

> Closed form is useful when available, but not always available.

---

## Closed Form vs Procedure

Closed form:

```text
x = (known expression)
```

Procedure:

```text
start with a guess
update the guess
repeat
```

Closed form gives the answer as an expression.

A procedure describes how to search or approximate.

> Closed form is direct; iteration is step-based.

---

## Final Summary

- A closed-form solution gives an answer directly.
- It uses a finite expression.
- Formulas are often closed-form solutions.
- Closed form does not require repeated approximation.
- Some problems require iterative methods instead.
