# Integers

## Definition

Integers are whole numbers that include negatives, zero, and positives.

They look like:

```text
..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
```

> Integers = whole numbers with direction around zero.

---

## Why Integers Extend Natural Numbers

Natural numbers can count:

```text
0, 1, 2, 3
```

But they cannot represent values below zero.

Integers allow:

```text
-1
-2
-3
```

This matters when representing change, loss, debt, offset, or direction.

> Integers let whole-number quantities move below zero.

---

## Simple Examples

Examples of integer values:

```text
temperature change: -5
account balance: -20
position offset: +3
counter value: 0
```

These values still move in whole steps.

They do not include fractions.

> Integers are still discrete values.

---

## Integers And Subtraction

Integers handle subtraction more naturally than natural numbers.

Example:

```text
2 - 5 = -3
```

The result is not natural, but it is an integer.

> Integers make subtraction closed over whole numbers.

---

## Integers On A Number Line

Integers can be placed on a number line:

```text
... -3 -2 -1 0 1 2 3 ...
```

Each neighboring integer is one step apart.

This makes integers useful for step-based movement.

> Integers represent discrete positions around zero.

---

## Final Summary

- Integers include negative whole numbers, zero, and positive whole numbers.
- Integers extend natural numbers.
- They are useful for change, direction, debt, and offset.
- Integers are discrete.
- Subtraction of integers stays inside integers.
