# Polynomial

## Definition

A polynomial is an algebraic expression built from variables, coefficients, addition, subtraction, multiplication, and nonnegative integer powers.

Example:

```text
3x^2 + 2x + 1
```

> Polynomial = sum of terms with nonnegative whole-number powers.

---

## Terms

A polynomial is made of terms.

Example:

```text
3x^2 + 2x + 1
```

Terms:

```text
3x^2
2x
1
```

Each term is a piece being added or subtracted.

> Terms are the building blocks of a polynomial.

---

## Coefficients

A coefficient is the number multiplying a variable part.

Example:

```text
3x^2
```

The coefficient is:

```text
3
```

In:

```text
2x
```

the coefficient is:

```text
2
```

> Coefficients control the size of terms.

---

## Powers

Polynomials use nonnegative integer powers.

Allowed:

```text
x^0
x^1
x^2
x^3
```

Not polynomial powers:

```text
x^-1
x^(1/2)
```

Those introduce different kinds of expressions.

> Polynomial powers must be whole-number powers greater than or equal to zero.

---

## Simple Examples

Polynomials:

```text
x + 1
x^2 + 3x + 2
5
4x^3 - x
```

Not polynomials:

```text
1 / x
sqrt(x)
2^x
```

> Polynomials have a restricted but useful structure.

---

## Final Summary

- A polynomial is an algebraic expression made from terms.
- Terms can contain variables raised to nonnegative integer powers.
- Coefficients multiply variable parts.
- Constants can be polynomial terms.
- `1 / x`, `sqrt(x)`, and `2^x` are not polynomials.
