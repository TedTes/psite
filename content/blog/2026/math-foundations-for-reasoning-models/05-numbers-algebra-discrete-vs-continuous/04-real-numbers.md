# Real Numbers

## Definition

Real numbers are the numbers that fill the number line.

They include:

- natural numbers
- integers
- rational numbers
- irrational numbers

Examples:

```text
-2
0
1 / 2
3.14
sqrt(2)
```

> Real numbers represent positions on a continuous number line.

---

## Rational And Irrational

Rational numbers can be written as ratios.

Example:

```text
1 / 2
```

Irrational numbers cannot be written as ratios of integers.

Example:

```text
sqrt(2)
```

Both rational and irrational numbers are real numbers.

> Real numbers include both exact ratios and non-ratio values.

---

## Measurement

Real numbers are useful for measurement.

Examples:

```text
length
weight
temperature
time
probability
position
```

Measurements often need values between whole numbers.

Example:

```text
2.5 meters
```

> Real numbers support quantities that vary between whole steps.

---

## Real Numbers Are Continuous

Between any two real numbers, there is another real number.

Example:

```text
2 and 3
```

Between them:

```text
2.5
2.25
2.125
```

and so on.

> Real numbers form a continuous line, not isolated steps.

---

## Approximation In Computation

Computers usually represent real numbers approximately.

Example:

```text
1 / 3 = 0.333...
```

A computer may store only a limited decimal-like approximation.

This does not change the mathematical idea.

It means implementation has limits.

> Real numbers are mathematically continuous, but computers store finite approximations.

---

## Final Summary

- Real numbers fill the number line.
- They include rationals and irrationals.
- They are useful for measurement.
- Real numbers are continuous.
- Between two real numbers there is always another real number.
- Computers usually approximate real values.
