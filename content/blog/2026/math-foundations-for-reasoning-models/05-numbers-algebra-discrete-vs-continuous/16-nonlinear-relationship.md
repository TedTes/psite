# Nonlinear Relationship

## Definition

A nonlinear relationship does not change at a constant rate.

Example:

```text
y = x^2
```

Values:

```text
x = 0 -> y = 0
x = 1 -> y = 1
x = 2 -> y = 4
x = 3 -> y = 9
```

The output changes faster as `x` grows.

> Nonlinear relationship = rate of change is not constant.

---

## Simple Example

For:

```text
y = x^2
```

Outputs:

```text
0, 1, 4, 9, 16
```

Differences:

```text
+1, +3, +5, +7
```

The differences are changing.

That means the relationship is not linear.

> Changing differences indicate nonlinear behavior.

---

## Nonlinear Does Not Mean Random

Nonlinear relationships can still be precise and rule-based.

Example:

```text
y = x^2
```

This is completely defined.

It is nonlinear because the rate of change is not constant.

> Nonlinear means not constant-rate, not unstructured.

---

## More Examples

Examples of nonlinear relationships:

```text
y = x^2
y = 2^x
y = 1 / x
```

Each follows a rule.

But none changes by the same fixed amount for each equal step of `x`.

> Nonlinear relationships can grow, shrink, curve, or saturate in different ways.

---

## Why Nonlinearity Matters

Nonlinearity allows richer behavior than straight-line change.

It can represent:

- acceleration
- compounding growth
- diminishing returns
- thresholds
- curved relationships

> Nonlinear structure is needed when constant-rate change is too simple.

---

## Final Summary

- A nonlinear relationship does not change at a constant rate.
- `y = x^2` is nonlinear.
- Nonlinear does not mean random.
- Nonlinear relationships still follow rules.
- Nonlinearity allows richer patterns than straight-line change.
