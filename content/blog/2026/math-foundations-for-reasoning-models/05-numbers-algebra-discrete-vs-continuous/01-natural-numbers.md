# Natural Numbers

## Definition

Natural numbers are the whole numbers used for counting.

In this series, we use:

```text
0, 1, 2, 3, 4, ...
```

Some contexts start natural numbers at `1`.

For computation and counting empty collections, including `0` is useful.

> Natural numbers = numbers for counting discrete quantities.

---

## Counting Objects

Natural numbers answer questions like:

```text
How many items?
How many steps?
How many nodes?
How many edges?
```

Examples:

```text
0 files
1 input
3 nodes
10 steps
```

> Natural numbers count things that come in separate units.

---

## Natural Numbers Are Discrete

Natural numbers do not include values between whole counts.

Between:

```text
2
3
```

there is no natural number.

You can have `2` nodes or `3` nodes, but not `2.5` nodes in a normal graph.

> Natural numbers are separated, countable values.

---

## Operations On Natural Numbers

Some operations stay inside natural numbers.

Example:

```text
2 + 3 = 5
2 * 3 = 6
```

But subtraction may leave the natural numbers.

Example:

```text
2 - 5 = -3
```

`-3` is not a natural number.

> Natural numbers are useful, but not closed under every operation.

---

## Why Natural Numbers Matter

Natural numbers are the starting point for numerical structure.

They let us count:

- symbols
- states
- steps
- nodes
- edges
- possible choices

This makes them directly useful for computation and graph structure.

> Counting is the first numerical layer on top of structure.

---

## Final Summary

- Natural numbers are whole numbers used for counting.
- This series includes `0` as a natural number.
- Natural numbers are discrete.
- They count separate units.
- Addition and multiplication stay natural.
- Subtraction can leave the natural numbers.
