# Rational Numbers

## Definition

A rational number is a number that can be written as a ratio of two integers.

Basic form:

```text
p / q
```

where:

```text
p is an integer
q is an integer
q is not 0
```

> Rational number = number expressible as an integer ratio.

---

## Simple Examples

Examples:

```text
1 / 2
3 / 4
-5 / 2
7 / 1
0 / 3
```

The number:

```text
7
```

is rational because it can be written as:

```text
7 / 1
```

> Every integer is rational, but not every rational number is an integer.

---

## Why The Denominator Cannot Be Zero

The denominator cannot be zero.

This is invalid:

```text
5 / 0
```

Division by zero does not define a valid rational number.

> Rational numbers require a nonzero denominator.

---

## Fractions And Exactness

Rational numbers are useful when a quantity can be divided exactly into parts.

Examples:

```text
1 / 2 of a value
3 / 4 of a path
2 / 5 of a group
```

Fractions preserve exact relationships.

The decimal `0.5` and the fraction `1 / 2` represent the same value, but the fraction shows the ratio directly.

> Rational numbers make part-whole relationships explicit.

---

## Decimal Form

Rational numbers have decimal forms that either terminate or repeat.

Examples:

```text
1 / 2 = 0.5
1 / 3 = 0.333...
1 / 4 = 0.25
```

> Rational decimals eventually stop or repeat.

---

## Final Summary

- Rational numbers can be written as `p / q`.
- `p` and `q` are integers.
- `q` cannot be zero.
- Every integer is rational.
- Rational numbers express exact ratios.
- Their decimal forms terminate or repeat.
